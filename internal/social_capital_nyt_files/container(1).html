    Is it Finally Time to Say
    Goodbye to the Résumé?

    To find candidates and diversify their talent pools, talent-acquisition leaders are scrapping the old way of doing things. “They’re breaking down barriers that have kept otherwise qualified candidates from being considered,” says Indeed’s Executive Vice President and General Manager, Raj Mukherjee. “This requires doing something that would have been unthinkable not long ago — eliminating résumé reviews entirely from the initial stages of recruiting.”

    The Benefits of
    Skills-Based Hiring

    Traditional resume criteria have limited access to jobs for many groups of people — like minorities, women, people without college degrees and people with criminal records — who have the skills those jobs require. While résumés are still an important tool for employers and job seekers, tools like Indeed Assessments, which focuses on the skills recruiters need for specific roles, offer job seekers a chance to get hired based on their unique skill sets.

    A Larger Pool of
    Qualified Candidates

    For talent-acquisition leaders, skills-based hiring could mean inviting candidates to take online assessments based on their skills, rather than simply reviewing a résumé. That way candidates can demonstrate the skills a role requires before the interview stage. “When employers don’t engage in skills-based hiring, they risk missing out on the opportunity to attract a larger pool of talented, hard-working, dependable candidates,” Mukherjee says.

    Better Opportunities for All

    When job seekers are given the right opportunities, they accept responsibilities that lead to raises, promotions and, eventually, better jobs. “For talent-acquisition leaders and employers, skills-based hiring means you’re more likely to hire people who perform well in their roles,” Mukherjee says. “The alignment between employee and job can provide multiple long-term benefits, including improved productivity, profitability and greater innovation.”
